indian institute of technology jodhpur
academic programmes at indian institute of technology jodhpur are designed to develop the highest calibre human resource capable of understanding the new patterns of knowledge creation across disciplines obliterating traditional boundaries between science humanities social sciences and engineering
iit jodhpur aims to produce quality professionals who would be able to address profound and wideranging societal challenges of the 21st century such as energy food water housing mobility and health
in addition to imparting scientific knowledge iit jodhpur endeavours to inculcate human qualities of courage integrity fairness humility and team effort among its graduates through curricular cocurricular and extracurricular activities on campus
the undergraduate academic programmes focus on developing temper for the life long process of learning creative thinking and exploring
the postgraduate academic programmes focus on developing deep understanding of the subject of study coupled with creative inquisitiveness and the ability to address and solve new problems with free and objective mind
the academic programmes are based on three tenets of semester system credit system and relative grading
these academic programmes are administered by duly constituted academic committee ac through the office of academics
the ac will be chaired by dean academics
in the absence of dean academics associate dean academics pgassociate dean academics ug will discharge the responsibilities of dean academics
the senate of the institute has formulated set of guidelines and rules to ensure high standard of performance as well as for smooth functioning of the academic programmes
within this broad set of guidelines subject to the approval of senate different programmes can include additional academic requirements as deemed necessary for that programme
these academic programmes are continuously monitored by the senate and make appropriate modifications improvements as and when necessary
the academic session normally runs from the end of july in one year to the middle of july in the next year
the academic session is divided into three parts two regular semesters and summer term as per the following timelines
the academic calendar for each academic session will be notified by the office of the dean academics before the start of the session
the academic calendar will specify the dates for registration last date for addingdeleting courses last date for withdrawal from courses date of midsemester examination last date for submission of midsemester grades date of endsemester examination last date for submission of endsemester grades date of summer term and such other dates as may be necessary
the institute follows credit system for all its academic programmes
each course has certain number of credits assigned to it depending on the lecture tutorial and laboratory contact hours per week
in general one lecture hour per week per semester carries one credit one tutorial hour per week per semester carries one credit and two to three laboratory hours per week per semester carry one credit
teaching and evaluation
each course is identified by unique code and course title
the course code consists of twoletter department code followed by threedigit number
the first digit indicates the level of the course
courses numbered are normally at the first year level at the second year level at the third year level at the fourth year level at the fifth year level at the sixth year level and are special courses
each course has specified number of credits
the credit structure is indicated as ltpc where stands for lecture hours per week for tutorial hours per week for practical hours per week and for total credits
courses are categorized as
core courses compulsory courses required for programme
elective courses courses that students can choose from specified list
open elective courses courses from other departments that students can choose
audit courses courses that students can attend without earning credits
every student must register for courses at the beginning of each semester as per the schedule announced in the academic calendar
registration is complete only after the student has enrolled in the required number of courses and the same has been approved by the faculty advisor
students may add or drop courses within the first two weeks of the semester as specified in the academic calendar
after the adddrop period students can withdraw from courses with grade until the date specified in the academic calendar
conduct of courses
each course is conducted by the instructor with the assistance of the required number of tutors as needed
the instructor is responsible for conducting the course evaluating the performance of students awarding the grades at the end of the semester and transmitting the grades as per subsection
the student shall have access to hisher answer papers of all the written examinations conducted for given course which may be shown to himher by the instructors concerned
the instructor must keep all evaluation records in his custody at least for the next six months
course approved by the senate of iit jodhpur can be conducted through either direct contact ii synchronized remote contact iii guided study mode or combination of these
in direct contact mode coordinator or instructor will have face to face physical interaction with the students
synchronized remote contact mode would require the faculty andor the students to be concurrently connected to the classroom interaction via electronic conferencing involving both audio and video communication
however there has to be direct interaction between faculty and students for limited hours typically of the stipulated contact hours
in guided study mode faculty would guide single or group of students to take up the study of an approved course via selflearning and discussionoriented contact sessions
this mode can be also adopted for enabling student to take up study of new course not yet approved by the senate under the supervision of faculty
all formal evaluation component applicable to courses being run through any one of the abovementioned modes will be conducted under direct physical supervision of faculty
all laboratory component of course requiring the use of the specialized facility will be conducted through direct contact
the instructors and tutors for all courses to be offered during the semester are allocated by the head of the department hosting the program
when faculty members of other departments are also required to participate in teaching particular course the allocation shall be done in consultation with the head of that department
components of evaluation
the evaluation of students performance in course shall be based on continuous assessment throughout the semester
the evaluation components may include quizzes assignments projects midsemester examination endsemester examination laboratory work and any other component as specified by the instructor
midsemester examination will be conducted as per the schedule specified in the academic calendar
the duration of the examination shall normally be two hours
endsemester examination will be conducted as per the schedule specified in the academic calendar
the duration of the examination shall normally be three hours
student who misses an examination due to valid reasons may be permitted to appear for makeup examination
the request for makeup examination must be submitted to the head of the department with supporting documents within three days of the missed examination
the performance of student in each course is evaluated on tenpoint scale using letter grades
the letter grades and their corresponding grade points are as follows
incomplete no grade point
satisfactory completion of course no grade point
unsatisfactory completion of course no grade point
withdrawal from course no grade point
award of grades
the instructor shall recommend the grades for all students registered in the course
the grades shall be submitted to the head of the department within the date specified in the academic calendar
the grades shall be reviewed and approved by the departmental undergraduate or postgraduate committee as applicable

grade point averages
semester grade point average
the semester grade point average sgpa is weighted average of the grades earned by student in all the courses credited by herhim and reflects herhis academic performance in the respective semester
if the grade points associated with the letter grades earned by student in courses registered during semester are g1 g2 gn and the corresponding credits c1 c2 cn then the sgpa is given by the following formula
sgpa g1c1 g2c2
gncn c1 c2
the sgpa is calculated on the basis of grades obtained in all courses except audit courses and courses in which sx grade is awarded registered in particular semester
cumulative grade point average
the cumulative grade point average cgpa indicates the overall academic performance of student in all the courses registered up to the latest completed semester
the cgpa is computed similarly as the sgpa considering all the courses in all semesters
the cgpa is calculated on the basis of all pass grades except audit courses and courses in which sx grade is awarded obtained in all completed semesters
the sgpa and cgpa calculation are based on credits earnedcompleted at iit jodhpur alone

grade card shall be issued to each student at the end of each semester and transcript at the end of the programme
iit jodhpur grade card and transcript will only indicate the courses credits and grades completed at iit jodhpur and the total number of credits without grades earned in other academic institutions in particular semester if applicable
